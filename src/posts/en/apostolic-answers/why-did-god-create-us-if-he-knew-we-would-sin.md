<p>If God knew that we were gonna mess up and that Adam and Eve were going to end up breaking His commandments and falling in the garden, why create us at all? Why go through the process of creating humanity if you know that eventually we're gonna bring about evil? Well, it's actually very simple.</p>
<br>
<p>If God truly is a God of love and He creates us strictly out of the goodness of His being, then He ultimately must give us free will, because you gotta be careful: if He removes from us the possibility of evil, then He's removing from us the possibility of freedom.</p>
<br>
<p>To remove freedom is to not leave room for the possibility of love, because love requires... the prerequisite for love is free will. And if He removes our free will, then unfortunately, He does not love us.</p>
<br>
<p>God must remain faithful to Himself and create us out of love. And in creating us out of love, He gives us freedom: freedom to love Him or freedom to turn against Him. And when we turn against Him, we commit evil.</p>
