<p>Some say that Jesus is just a prophet, not God, and that the Bible apparently says so. They quote passages like Matthew 21:11, Luke 7:16, Luke 24:19 and other passages where the people around Jesus speak and describe Him as a prophet.</p>
<br>
<p>Firstly, the Messiah, the Christ of God was always supposed to be prophetic in His workings. So, it's no surprise that part of Jesus' ministry is to be prophetic. That doesn't make Him any less divine. This is like saying the president of a country, who is also a citizen and has responsibilities to act like a citizen, cannot be president, because he's a citizen. That's silly!</p>
<br>
<p>Secondly, why only reference the passages that say people describe Jesus as a prophet? Why not reference the full passages where Jesus is clearly seen as God almighty?</p>
<br>
<p>In Luke 7, Jesus raises the dead and people say: God has visited us. In Matthew 21, He enters into Jerusalem and the people offer praises of Hosanna! to Him, praises worthy only of God.</p>
