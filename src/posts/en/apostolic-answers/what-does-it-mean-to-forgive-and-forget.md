<p>What does it mean for us to forgive? Are we expected to forget the offense that the person has committed against us? Are we expected somehow to erase the memory from our minds of what it is that has happened? Of course not! That's not what forgiveness is.</p>
<br>
<p>Forgiveness doesn't imply the forgetfulness of the event, as if somehow you're supposed to alter history as it was recorded in your mind. But rather, forgiveness is being able to look past the sin in order for you to be able to see the person. If my brother has committed a sin against me, when I look at him, I don't simply want to see him as a person who has committed offense: I want to see him as my brother whom I love.</p>
<br>
<p>So, forgiveness is the lifting away of that barrier that has been created between him and I, so that I can see him for who he is: my brother, my loved one, the person who Christ died for, this person that I am called to cherish and to see as a full human being created in God's image and likeness.</p>
