<p>Let's talk about peace for just a second. Peace sometimes is described by people as if it's a description of what is happening outside of them, their environment, their circumstance. But in reality, when Christians talk about peace, it's a reflection of what is happening inside of us. Let me explain what it is I'm trying to say.</p>
<br>
<p>In the passage where the Lord Jesus Christ is about to send forth His apostles into the world, the Great Commission, He says: My peace I give to you, my peace I leave with you, not as the world gives do I give to you. What is it, or rather, who is it that came to us when the Lord Jesus Christ ascended and we received Him? The Holy Spirit! Peace, to us, is the Holy Spirit Himself.</p>
<br>
<p>As a matter of fact, St. Cyril of Alexandria comments on this very passage in Scripture and talks about how it is that the author of peace, the peace that we receive, is the Holy Spirit Himself. So, the next time you're looking for peace, don't just focus on changing your environment. Focus on entering into dialogue with the person of the Holy Spirit.</p>
