<p>Want you to imagine for just a moment that you had three water droplets; three drops of water. You bring them together. When they come together, those three water droplets become one. They don't remain divided. Why? Because they share the same essence, the same substance.</p>
<br>
<p>While this is nothing more than a very finite example, a failed example, this points to something that we can understand in the Holy Trinity. We are oftentimes accused of worshipping three Gods: Father, Son, Holy Spirit. This is simply not true. We don't worship three: we worship one.</p>
<br>
<p>But the Father, Son and the Holy Spirit, three hypostases, three divine persons share the same essence. In sharing that essence, they are one godhead.</p>
