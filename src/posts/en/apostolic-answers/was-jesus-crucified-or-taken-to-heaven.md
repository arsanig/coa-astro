<p>Some people would like to argue that Jesus was never actually crucified or put to death. Obviously, this is false. All four of the Gospels give a detailed account of Jesus' crucifixion and His death on the cross. Even the Epistles. There are nine or ten of the New Testament books that all reference the crucifixion and the death of Our Lord Jesus Christ on the cross.</p>
<br>
<p>People will turn around and say: We can't trust your Bible. Okay, let's get some proof outside the Bible:</p>
<br>
<p>Josephus, 1 st century historian, in his book The Antiquities of the Jews, he talks about how it is that Jesus was put to death under Pontius Pilate. That same statement is echoed again by another Roman historian by the name of Tacitus who references how it is that Pontius Pilate put Him to death.</p>
<br>
<p>Then you have Mara bar Serapion. In his letter, in 73 A.D., he talks about how it is that Jesus was a king of the Jews who was put to death by His own people. Finally, and a very important reference: the Babylonian Talmud, which is a Sanhedrin tractate, contains a passage that refers to Jesus's crucifixion.</p>
