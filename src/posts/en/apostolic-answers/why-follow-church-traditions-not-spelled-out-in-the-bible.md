<p>Should we follow the traditions of the Church if they are not explicitly written down in Scripture? Many people ask this question. They love to make the statement and say what? If it's written in the Bible, I'll do it, but if you can't show it to me in Scripture, then there's no reason for me to follow it.</p>
<br>
<p>Well, hold on, it's not that simple! You see, we know that historically, in the early Church, the apostles who received instructions from our Lord Jesus Christ handed down many of these traditions to us orally. They weren't necessarily written down. We even know that the Church predates the Scriptures. Long before the Gospels were written down and spread across the globe, the Church was fully alive and handing down its teachings and its traditions through oral tradition, through discipleship.</p>
<br>
<p>St. John himself, in the last chapter of his Gospel, what does he say? He says the Lord Himself said many other things and did many other things that we did not write down. And had we written them down, the world itself would not be able to contain all the books we would have written. Think about that!</p>
